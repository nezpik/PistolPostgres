# PistolPostgres

**Precision Intelligent Self-evolving & Optimizing Layer for Postgres**

**Version:** 0.1 (Initial Blueprint)  
**Date:** July 2026  
**Status:** Design Phase  
**Core Principle:** Reliable self-modification through controlled, explainable, evolutionary intelligence on a battle-tested Postgres foundation.

---

## 1. Executive Summary & Vision

PistolPostgres is a hybrid, middle-ground database architecture that transforms a standard Postgres (or Supabase) instance into a **self-improving system**. It keeps the proven Postgres storage engine, MVCC, WAL/recovery, query planner, and concurrency model untouched for maximum reliability, while adding a dedicated **Evolution Intelligence Layer** that continuously analyzes workload, proposes structural improvements, evaluates them safely, and applies beneficial changes in a controlled, auditable, and reversible manner.

### The Core Vision
A Postgres database that **gets better over time by itself** — adapting its physical design (indexes, partitions, materialized views) and safe schema extensions to real usage patterns, without requiring constant manual DBA work or risky ad-hoc migrations.

### Why "PistolPostgres"?
- **Precision**: Every change is targeted, data-driven, and evaluated before application ("one precise shot").
- **Intelligent**: LLM/agent-driven proposals combined with evolutionary/stochastic search.
- **Controlled & Reliable**: Strong safety rails, provenance, sandbox testing, and rollback — built on Postgres's rock-solid foundation.
- **Differentiating Hook**: Evolutionary non-determinism in the *proposal* phase + full auditability of self-modification, something missing from both traditional advisors and full autonomous databases.

This directly addresses the original goal: a database with a **precise, striking, technically differentiating value** that solves real problems (performance drift + schema debt) in an intelligent yet controlled way.

## 2. Problem Statement & Value Proposition

### The Problem
Modern applications (especially edtech, AI-powered, or fast-iterating products) suffer from:
- **Performance drift**: Query patterns change as features grow (new Massar integrations, realtime social features, AI agent queries, multi-tenant scaling). Manually chosen indexes and physical layout become suboptimal.
- **Schema/physical design debt**: Manual migrations are slow, error-prone, and lag behind application changes. Developers avoid complex optimizations.
- **Lack of observability into "why" changes happen**: Most tools give recommendations without rich context or provenance.
- **No safe automation**: Existing advisors (pganalyze, Azure autonomous tuning, hypopg-based tools) are mostly advisory or greedy/local. Full autonomous systems (Oracle) are proprietary and heavy.

### The Differentiating Solution (PistolPostgres Hook)
PistolPostgres provides **controlled evolutionary self-optimization**:
- Background intelligence continuously explores better physical layouts using stochastic/evolutionary methods (escaping local optima that simple recommenders hit).
- Every proposal is rigorously evaluated in sandbox conditions.
- Changes are applied safely (preferring online DDL), fully logged with AI + metric rationale, and automatically monitored for regressions.
- The system is **agent-native**: Your Hermes Agent can participate in or drive the evolution loop.
- Result: The database "sharpens itself" over time — delivering better performance, lower costs, and less developer toil — while remaining trustworthy.

**Primary Use Case Alignment**: Perfect for your Supabase-backed edtech application with 85+ tables, complex RLS, realtime features, and growing multi-tenant workloads. It reduces the "DBA tax" as the product scales and evolves.

**Design Principles**:
- **Postgres owns data integrity and durability** — never bypass it.
- **Evolution is observable and queryable** — the "self-changing" behavior lives in regular Postgres tables.
- **Safety before autonomy** — graduated rollout from advisory → auto-safe-ops → broader evolution.
- **Agent-first** — Hermes is a first-class citizen, not an afterthought.
- **Evolutionary non-determinism** is confined to the low-risk proposal phase.

## 4. Detailed Component Breakdown

### 4.1 Telemetry Collector
- Primary sources: `pg_stat_statements`, `pg_stat_user_tables/indexes`, `pg_stat_activity`, table bloat stats, custom query logging where needed.
- Enrichment: Capture representative workload samples or statistical summaries (query fingerprints, frequency, cost, cardinality estimates).
- Storage: Time-series or aggregated data in dedicated `telemetry_*` tables (or exported to the intelligence service).
- Goal: Provide rich, queryable context for proposals without heavy overhead.

### 4.2 Proposal & Evolutionary Engine (The Intelligence Core)
- **LLM Component**: Hermes Agent (or dedicated local model) receives structured context (schema subset, stats, sample queries, goals/SLOs, current genome state) and outputs candidate changes in structured JSON.
- **Evolutionary / Stochastic Layer**:
  - Maintains a small population of "DB genomes" (combinations of indexes, partition keys, materialized views, clustering strategies).
  - Applies mutations (add/remove index, change partition column, adjust MV definition) and limited crossovers.
  - Uses stochastic sampling or simple genetic algorithm steps to explore the space.
  - Fitness function is multi-objective: estimated latency improvement, storage cost, write amplification, maintenance overhead, risk score, alignment with business SLOs.
  - Inspiration from your RUIN framework: Stochastic processes can model "performance ruin" probability or workload evolution to decide when aggressive vs conservative exploration is warranted.
- Output: Ranked list of high-quality proposals with rationale, predicted impact, and confidence.

**Example Structured Proposal (JSON)**:
```json
{
  "proposal_id": "evo-2026-07-01-001",
  "type": "index_creation",
  "target_table": "student_progress",
  "ddl": "CREATE INDEX CONCURRENTLY idx_student_progress_student_id_created_at ON student_progress(student_id, created_at DESC);",
  "rationale": "Hot query pattern on student dashboards + Massar syncs. High filter + sort cardinality. Estimated 40-60% latency reduction on top 5 queries.",
  "predicted_impact": { "p99_latency_reduction_pct": 52, "storage_mb": 48, "write_amp_increase_pct": 3 },
  "fitness_score": 0.87,
  "confidence": 0.91,
  "triggering_queries": ["qfp-abc123", "qfp-def456"],
  "genome_variants_tested": 12
}
```

### 4.3 Evaluation Harness (Safety Critical)
- **hypopg** for zero-cost hypothetical index and (limited) other structure testing.
- Sampled `EXPLAIN (ANALYZE, BUFFERS)` on representative queries or traces.
- For higher-risk changes: Automatic creation/use of Supabase preview branches or logical replication replicas for realistic replay.
- Regression detection thresholds (e.g., never apply if any regression > X% on key queries).
- Cost modeling: Combine Postgres cost model with custom heuristics and LLM-estimated business value.

### 4.4 Policy & Decision Engine
Declarative policies stored in Postgres (editable table or YAML synced):
- Auto-apply rules (e.g., "CREATE INDEX if predicted improvement > 30% AND no regression on write path AND table < 50GB").
- Protected objects (never touch core auth/RLS tables automatically).
- Resource limits (max indexes per table, max storage added per day).
- Time windows and concurrency controls.
- Approval requirements (human or Hermes sign-off for structural schema changes).
- Graduated autonomy levels.

### 4.5 Provenance & Evolution Catalog (The "Memory" of Self-Changes)
All evolution activity is first-class data in Postgres. Key tables:

```sql
-- Core evolution history (immutable audit log)
CREATE TABLE pistol_evolution_history (
    id BIGSERIAL PRIMARY KEY,
    applied_at TIMESTAMPTZ DEFAULT now(),
    proposal_id TEXT,
    change_type TEXT,                    -- 'index', 'partition', 'materialized_view', 'schema_extension'
    target_object TEXT,
    ddl_executed TEXT,
    rationale TEXT,                      -- AI + metric explanation
    before_metrics JSONB,
    after_metrics JSONB,
    actual_impact JSONB,
    rollback_ddl TEXT,
    triggered_by TEXT,                   -- 'auto', 'hermes', 'manual'
    genome_context JSONB,
    CONSTRAINT pistol_evolution_history_proposal_id_key UNIQUE (proposal_id)
);

-- Current active "genome" state for evolutionary tracking
CREATE TABLE pistol_current_genome (
    id BIGSERIAL PRIMARY KEY,
    updated_at TIMESTAMPTZ DEFAULT now(),
    active_indexes JSONB,                -- structured representation
    active_partitions JSONB,
    active_mvs JSONB,
    fitness_snapshot JSONB
);

-- Proposals queue / history
CREATE TABLE pistol_proposals (
    id TEXT PRIMARY KEY,
    created_at TIMESTAMPTZ DEFAULT now(),
    status TEXT,                         -- 'proposed', 'evaluating', 'approved', 'applied', 'rejected', 'rolled_back'
    proposal_json JSONB,
    evaluation_results JSONB,
    policy_decision JSONB,
    applied_history_id BIGINT REFERENCES pistol_evolution_history(id)
);
```

These tables enable powerful queries like: "Show me all changes that improved student dashboard p99 by >30% in the last 90 days" or "What is the current genome fitness trend?"

### 4.6 Apply & Feedback Loop
- **Apply Path**: Prefer `CREATE INDEX CONCURRENTLY`, online DDL where supported. Use advisory locks or low-traffic scheduling. For complex changes, leverage logical replication or Supabase branching.
- **Post-Apply Monitoring**: Automatic comparison of key metrics (from telemetry) against predictions. Regression triggers alert + optional automatic rollback (via stored `rollback_ddl`).
- **Learning Signal**: Outcomes feed back into the proposal engine (improves future LLM prompts and evolutionary fitness models).

## 5. Safety, Control & Reliability Mechanisms

PistolPostgres is designed with **defense-in-depth**:

1. **Evaluation before any change** — Nothing reaches production without hypopg + sampling or branch testing.
2. **Policy gates** — Hard limits + human/agent approval for higher-risk operations.
3. **Transactional & online-first** — Leverage Postgres primitives; avoid blocking operations.
4. **Full provenance & immutability** — Evolution history cannot be tampered with easily.
5. **Automatic regression detection + rollback** — Closed feedback loop.
6. **Resource & blast-radius protection** — Policies prevent runaway behavior.
7. **Crash safety** — All state lives in Postgres; recovery follows normal Postgres rules. Evolution steps are idempotent or have clear undo paths where possible.
8. **Graduated autonomy** — Start in fully advisory mode; increase automation only after proven stability.
9. **Testing discipline** — Property-based testing of proposal generation, crash injection during simulated evolution steps, workload replay suites.

Data integrity is **never compromised** for the sake of self-modification.

## 7. Non-Deterministic & Evolutionary Aspects

The "non-deterministic" quality lives primarily in the **proposal generation phase**:
- Stochastic mutations and population-based search over physical design combinations.
- Probabilistic elements in fitness scoring or workload forecasting (can draw from your RUIN stochastic modeling work).
- Multiple candidate futures are explored cheaply; only the best, safest survivors are promoted to evaluation/apply.

This gives better global optimization than pure greedy or single-LLM-shot recommenders, while the control layer ensures only safe, explainable changes ever touch production data.

## 9. Technical Choices & Trade-offs

- **Language for Intelligence Layer**: Rust (preferred for safety + performance) or Go (faster iteration). Python acceptable for early prototypes.
- **Communication**: Postgres functions/RPC for simple ops; dedicated HTTP/gRPC API for complex evolutionary workflows.
- **Evaluation**: hypopg (proven, lightweight) + Supabase branching (powerful for realistic tests).
- **Storage of Evolution State**: Regular Postgres tables (queryable, transactional, leverages your existing infra).
- **Trade-off**: Slightly higher operational complexity (one extra service) vs. massive gains in reliability and development velocity compared to from-scratch. Much deeper self-modification capability than pure external advisors.

## 10. Risks & Mitigations

| Risk                        | Mitigation                                                                 |
|-----------------------------|----------------------------------------------------------------------------|
| AI proposes harmful change  | Mandatory evaluation harness + conservative policies + rollback capability |
| Performance regression      | Pre-apply prediction + post-apply automatic detection + rollback           |
| Storage bloat               | Policy limits + fitness function penalizes unnecessary structures          |
| Complexity / maintenance    | Start narrow (indexes), add features gradually; full provenance aids debugging |
| Concurrency / locking issues| Prefer CONCURRENTLY operations; schedule during low-traffic windows        |
| Data integrity during evo   | Never bypass Postgres transaction/recovery model; evolution steps are additive/safe |
